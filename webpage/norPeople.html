Recent Events That Changed Lives

May 10-12, 2024
The Mother's Day Storm

The most powerful solar storm in over 20 years disrupted GPS systems worldwide. Farmers couldn't plant crops with precision equipment. Delivery drivers got lost. Emergency services struggled with navigation. Power grids in several U.S. states experienced voltage fluctuations.

February 2022
The Satellite Internet Crisis

A geomagnetic storm knocked out satellite internet for millions of rural users across North America. Students couldn't attend online classes. Remote workers missed deadlines. Telemedicine appointments were cancelled. Some areas were without connectivity for over 48 hours.

September 2017
Hurricane Season Blackout

During an already devastating hurricane season, a solar storm disrupted emergency communication systems in the Caribbean. Radio blackouts prevented coordination of rescue efforts. GPS errors complicated evacuation routes. The compound disaster showed our vulnerability.

March 1989
The Quebec Blackout

Six million people lost power for 9 hours in Quebec when a geomagnetic storm overwhelmed the power grid. Hospitals switched to backup generators. Subway trains stopped mid-route. People were trapped in elevators. The event cost an estimated $2 billion and remains a warning of what could happen again.
